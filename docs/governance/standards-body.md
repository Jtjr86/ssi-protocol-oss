# SSI Standards Body — Structure & Responsibilities

## 1. Overview

The **SSI Standards Body** is the central governance authority for the SSI Protocol.

It consists of:
- **SSI Standards Board** — Strategic oversight and final decision authority
- **Safety & Ethics Review Board (SERB)** — Safety and ethics validation
- **Technical Working Groups** — Domain-specific development and review

## 2. Standards Board

### Composition
- 7-11 members representing diverse stakeholder groups
- Seats allocated to: enterprises, developers, researchers, regulators, civil society
- 3-year terms, staggered to ensure continuity
- Chair elected by board members

### Responsibilities
- Approve protocol roadmap and major milestones
- Ratify specifications and breaking changes
- Appoint working group chairs and SERB members
- Maintain registry of governance authorities
- Resolve disputes and appeals

### Decision Rules
- Quorum: 2/3 of members
- Approval: Simple majority for operational matters, 2/3 for protocol changes
- Transparency: All votes and reasoning published

## 3. Safety & Ethics Review Board (SERB)

### Composition
- 5-7 experts in AI safety, ethics, and risk
- Independent of commercial interests
- Appointed by Standards Board for 2-year terms

### Responsibilities
- Review safety implications of all protocol changes
- Validate threat models and mitigation strategies
- Provide ethics guidance for governance policies
- Publish safety bulletins and advisories
- Audit reference implementations for safety invariants

### Authority
- Can block protocol changes on safety grounds
- Can issue emergency safety patches
- Reports directly to Standards Board

## 4. Technical Working Groups

### Formation
- Created by Standards Board to address specific protocol domains
- Examples: Gateway WG, Kernel WG, RPX WG, Governance WG, Security WG

### Responsibilities
- Draft and refine specifications
- Build and maintain reference implementations
- Review community RFCs in their domain
- Ensure backward compatibility
- Coordinate with other working groups

### Membership
- Open to anyone with relevant expertise
- Chair appointed by Standards Board
- Decisions by rough consensus

## 5. Transparency & Accountability

- All meetings documented with public minutes
- RFCs and decisions published on ssi-protocol.org
- Annual report on governance activities
- Code of conduct for all participants
