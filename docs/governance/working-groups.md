## 1. Overview

SSI **Working Groups** are focused teams that develop and maintain specific aspects of the protocol.

Each working group:
- Has a clear scope and charter
- Is led by an appointed chair
- Operates through open participation
- Delivers specifications, reference code, and documentation

## 2. Current Working Groups

### Protocol Architecture WG
**Scope:** Core protocol design, kernel, gateway, RPX format  
**Deliverables:** Architecture specs, interface definitions, compatibility guidelines

### Governance & Policy WG
**Scope:** Governance envelopes, policy languages, constitutional frameworks  
**Deliverables:** Envelope specs, policy authoring tools, governance templates

### Security & Cryptography WG
**Scope:** Audit trail integrity, signatures, privacy, threat mitigation  
**Deliverables:** Security specs, cryptographic standards, vulnerability assessments

### Developer Experience WG
**Scope:** SDKs, APIs, integration patterns, tooling  
**Deliverables:** Client libraries, quickstart guides, tutorials, reference apps

### Regulatory & Compliance WG
**Scope:** Alignment with regulatory frameworks, audit standards  
**Deliverables:** Compliance guides, regulatory mapping, certification processes

### Enterprise Adoption WG
**Scope:** Enterprise deployment patterns, scalability, operations  
**Deliverables:** Deployment guides, best practices, production checklists

## 3. Working Group Lifecycle

### Formation
1. Proposal submitted to Standards Board
2. Charter defining scope, goals, deliverables
3. Chair appointed
4. Initial members recruited

### Operation
- Regular meetings (cadence defined in charter)
- Public minutes and decision logs
- RFC drafting and review
- Coordination with other WGs

### Deliverables
- Specifications published as RFCs
- Reference implementations contributed to official repos
- Documentation integrated into ssi-protocol.org

### Sunset
- When scope is completed or superseded
- Assets transferred to maintenance mode
- Members rotate to other WGs or new initiatives

## 4. Participation

### How to Join
1. Attend open meetings (schedules on website)
2. Contribute to discussions and RFCs
3. Volunteer for specific tasks
4. No formal membership required

### Expectations
- Constructive participation
- Respect for code of conduct
- Technical merit over politics
- Commitment to protocol's mission

## 5. Coordination

- **Cross-WG sync:** Monthly coordination meetings
- **Standards Board liaison:** Each WG reports quarterly
- **SERB review:** Safety-critical changes reviewed by SERB
- **Community feedback:** Public comment periods for major changes
